INDIAN FOLK DANCE

India is a land of the most diverse cultures and traditions in the world. India have a vast range of Dance forms weather it is folk or classical dance. Here the dialect change almost after 100 km, so does the folk dances style, dress, artists, etc change. We also have four season so we have dances for different season. There is one dance in almost every state for the time of harvest. Folk dances are form of expression, performed to reflect the happiness, sorrow and different mood of the society.

The gondhal is performed as a ritualistic folk dance to appease the goddesses Renuka, Bhavani or Amba.

BHARATNATTYAM(A Tamilian Folk Dance) BHARATNATTYAM(A Tamilian Folk Dance)

It traces its origins back to the Natyashastra,an ancient treatise on theatre written by the mythic priest Bharata.

DUMHAL(A Kashmiri Folk Dance) DUMHAL(A Kashmiri Folk Dance)

Dumhal is a dance performed in the Indian territory of Jammu and Kashmir by the Watal tribe.

PURULIA CHHAU(An Eastern India Folk Dance) PURULIA CHHAU(An Eastern India Folk Dance)

Chau found in three styles:the Purulia Chau of Bengal,the Seraikella Chau of Jharkhand, and the Mayurbhanj Chau of Odisha.

YAKSHAGANA(A Karnataki Folk Dance) YAKSHAGANA(A Karnataki Folk Dance)

A traditional Indian theatre form,that combines dance, music, dialogue, costume, make-up, and stage techniques with a unique style.

GARBA(A Folk Dance of Gujarat) GARBA(A Folk Dance of Gujarat)

It is performed during the nine-day Hindu festival Navaratri,Either the lamp(Diya for Goddess Amba) or an image of the Goddess, Durga is placed in middle of concentric rings as an object of veneration.

BHAVAI(A Rajasthani Folk Dance) BHAVAI(A Rajasthani Folk Dance)

Bhav means universe and Aai means mother, so it may be considered an art form dedicated to the Mother of the Universe, Amba. Bhavai is also known as Vesha or Swang, which literally means 'get-up'.

The dance weaves in aspects related to their lifestyle and occupation which is fishing.Me Dolker, Aga Pori Sambhaal, Dol Doltai, Paru Go Paru and Valav Re Nakva are some of the popular songs that dancers perform to.
