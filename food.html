INDIAN FOOD CULTURE

Indian cuisine consists of a variety of regional and traditional cuisines native to the Indian subcontinent. Given the diversity in soil, climate, culture, ethnic groups, and occupations, these cuisines vary substantially and use locally available spices, herbs, vegetables, and fruits. Indian food is also heavily influenced by religion, in particular Hinduism, cultural choices and traditions.

Paneer Tikka Masala Paneer Tikka Masala(North Indian Cuisine)

Paneer tikka masala is an Indian dish of marinated paneer cheese served in a spiced gravy.

Rasmalai(A Sweet dish from Bengal) Rasmalai(A Sweet dish from Bengal)

Ras malai or rossomalai or rasamalei is a dessert originating from the Indian subcontinent.

Hyderabadi Biryani(A Hyderabadi cuisine) Hyderabadi Biryani(A Hyderabadi cuisine)

Hyderabadi biryani is a variety of biryani from Hyderabad, India.It is prepared from rice using the dum method of cooking.

Mumbaicha VadaPav(Famous Fastfood of Maharashtra) VadaPav(Famous Fastfood of Maharashtra)

Vada pav, alternatively spelt wada pao, is a vegetarian fast food dish native to the state of Maharashtra.

Lassi(A Drink from Punjab) Lassi(A Drink from Punjab)

Lassi is a popular traditional dahi-based drink(yoghurt) that originated in the Indian Subcontinent.

Dosa(South- Indian Cuisine) Dosa(South Indian Cuisine)

A dosa is a rice pancake, originating from South India, made from a fermented batter predominantly consisting of lentils and rice.

Chicken Handi Chicken Handi (North- Indian Cuisine)

Chicken handi is a quintessential Indian dish found in almost all restaurant menu in India! ... The name 'chicken handi' implies that the chicken is cooked in an earthen pot of round shape which is called 'handi' in Hindi.

Daal Baati Churma Daal Baati Churma (Rajasthani Cuisine)

Dal baati is an Indian dish of dal and baati. It is popular in Rajasthan, Maharashtra's Khandesh and Vidarbha region, Gujarat, Uttar Pradesh and Madhya Pradesh. Dal is prepared using tuvaar dal, chana dal, mung dal, moth dal, or urad dal.

Solkadhi(A Drink from Konkan) Solkadhi(A Drink from Konkan)

Solkadhi is a type of drink, an appetizer originating from the Indian subcontinent, usually eaten with rice or sometimes drunk after or along with the meals. Popular in Goa and the Maharashtra's coastal region, it is made from coconut milk and kokum.
